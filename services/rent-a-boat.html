Where can you go?

You are free to go anywhere you want, just mind the time you need to return to make sure you are back in time. Shown below are some suggestions. If you would like to visit all of the below, make sure to book our full day tour with skipper.

1. Blue cave

Dive underwater and enter the famous blue cave to swim in the clear blue water. We will provide all the snorkels, so don't worry about that. The cave is only reachable by a boat, so don't miss out!

2. Three green caves

Just a super short travel from the Blue cave there are 3 additional green caves. The first one is big and easy, just swim inside and warm up for the next ones! The second cave is more interesting as it is darker and smaller. The third cave is a long and narrow channel which you can go thought and exit on the other side and then come back swimming around. It is necessary to dive a couple of times to go through. At one point you can even dive off the course to your right and dive out in a small cave with shallow sand bottom and no roof.

3. Kalamota

A small fishing village where you can walk around and explore. There is a small shop and a coffe bar by the sea.

4. Šunj beach

The most famous sandy beach in Dubrovnik, located on the island of Lopud. It has a big shallow area which makes is great for kids. There are a couple of bars and restaurants, sun beds and umbrellas, and paddle cars to rent.

5. Lopud village

Explore the other side of the Lopud island. From here you can visit the Dominican and Franciscan monasteries, Đorđić-Mayneri Park and small churches. Or you can enjoy some more swimming and snorkeling on other smaller sandy beaches. Here you can also have lunch at one of the restaurants by the sea.

6. Šipan

The largest Elaphiti island. Here you can explore another small fishing village called Suđurađ.

7. Sikirica

Sikirica cove offers a piece of paradise for swimming and snorkelling. Clear blue sea with shallow and rich sea vegetation makes it a perfect place to swim and snorkel.
